About

I am Research Fellow in Economics at PSAE (INRAE, University Paris-Saclay) and a Teaching Professor at École Polytechnique. My broad research agenda focuses on the behavioral determinants of health-related decisions. My recent work lies at the intersection of health and behavioral economics and epidemiology, and aims to measure individual psychological characteristics at the population level in order to predict engagement in primary and secondary prevention behaviors.

To this end, I have developed new survey-based tools to measure risk, time, and ambiguity attitudes, and leverage state-of-the-art methods from decision theory and experimental economics.

For the 2025/2026 academic year, I am Visiting Scholar in the Department of Economics at the University of California, Santa Barbara (UCSB).
